Chance of Return

Raid mechanic


We were facing two big problems in our Factions servers. The first problem was that the players would most likely quit after being raided. The second problem was that some players would explode their own blocks, just to dont let their opponents get them.

To give context: the most valuable items in the server were blocks that spawned monsters. You could steal those blocks from other players by exploding them. When one of those blocks got exploded, there was a chance of it dropping, and a chance of it just disappearing (this acted as a money sink). If you destroyed your own block, it would always disappear.

We considered implementing an insurance system for the players to get back their blocks in case they were raided, but we didnt end up using it. Since it would be possible to opt out of the system, some players cerainly would just lose their blocks in raids, and be frustrated for not having saved some money or activated the insurance.

Eventually we came up with a great solution that solved both problems. Now, when a block is exploded by an opponent, it has a chance of dropping, a chance of disappearing, and a chance of returning to its original owners. This made so that it wasnt worth it to explode your own blocks anymore, since by doing it you would have no chance of getting them back. This also made it almost impossible for players to lose all of their blocks in a raid, making them more likely to keep playing the game.

Lost Fortress

In-game event

This was an in-game event made for an MMO server. Our main objective with this was to create a point of massive PvP in the world.

Every few days a fortress would spawn in a random location, and inside of it the players could find a variety of rare items. In order to get inside the fortress, the players had to explode its walls using TNTs and other explosives.

Multiple guilds would try to get into the fortress as fast as possible, and they also would also try to sabotage other guilds, making so that the players would have to defend their attack structures to get inside of the fortress successfuly.

In order to support different playstyles during the raid (like sneak invading or fake invading), the fortress had to be very big, but that decision led us to some challenges.

Because of the size of the fortress, we had to create a dedicated world for the event, since in the default world it was almost impossible to place it without destroying player buildings. We also had to tweak the travel distance of the TNTs in that world, so that they could reach the deepest layers of the fortress.

In the design process we also had to make sure that the rewards found inside the fortress would be worth the time and resources put into the raid.
